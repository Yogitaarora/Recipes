DESCRIPTION :

Soya Manchurian is a delicious Asian recipe where soya chunks are tossed in a sweet and spicy Chinese sauce. Goes best with hakka noodles and fried rice.

INGREDIENTS :

  • 2 cups Soya Chunks
  • 2 tablespoons Oil
  • 1 teaspoon minced Garlic
  • 1 teaspoon minced Ginger
  • 3 Green Chillies, slit in half
  • 1 large Onion, cubed
  • 1 Bell Pepper, cubed
  • 3 tablespoons Corn Flour, divided
  • 3 tablespoons Light Soy Sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Chilli Sauce or hot sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Tomato Ketchup
  • Chopped green onions for topping

INSTRUCTIONS :

  1. Soak soya chunks in enough hot water to cover them and set aside for 15 minutes. Once soaked for 15 minutes, drain and squeeze excess water.
  2. Toss the soya chunks in a tablespoon of corn flour.
  3. Heat a tablespoon oil in a large pan and add the soya chunks. Fry them on all sides for 3-4 minutes and remove from pan. In the same pan, add remaining oil and stir fry garlic, ginger, green chillies and onion on high heat for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the bell peppers and soya chunks to the pan and toss well. 
  5. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, chilli sauce, ketchup, remaining cornflour and 3/4 cup water and add this to the pan. Keep stirring till the sauce comes to a boil and thickens. Turn off the flame, top with sliced green onions and serve hot.
